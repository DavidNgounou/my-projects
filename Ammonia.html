LABORATORY PREPARATION OF AMMONIA

AIM: Laboratory preparation of Ammonium chloride on calcium hydroxide.

Requirement: round bottom flask, delivery tube, gas jar, clamped stand, drying tower, ammonium chloride and calcium hydroxide, Bunsen burner for heating.

Procedure:

  1. Ammonia is prepared in laboratory bey heating a mixture of ammonium salt and slaked lime (calcium hydroxide)
    Slaked lime is used because its non-volatile, cheap and not deliquescent.
  2. The ammonium chloride and calcium hydroxide are both solid, so should be thoroughly grind into a mixture of finely powdered to provide large surface area for reaction.
  3. The mixture is placed in a slanted round-bottom flask
    4. This is done so as to prevent water vapor formed during the process form trickling back into the hottest part of the flask and crack it.
  4. The mixture is heated.
  5. The Ammonia gas evolved on heating is dried by passing it through a drying tower or U-tube containing quick lime to dry the gas.
  6. The ammonia gas is then collected by upward delivery, since it's less dense than air.

2NH4Cl(s) + Ca(OH)2 (s)--------------> CaCl2 + 2H2O(l) + 2NH3 (g)

TEST FOR AMMONIA

  • Ammonia gas has a characteristic of chocking smell and turns damp red litmus paper blue.
  • Ammonia gs reacts with concentrated HCl acid to produce solid white dense fumes of ammonium chloride.

NB: Ammonia is used as a reducing agent. It reduces black copper (II) oxide to brown mettalic copper and itself oxidize to nitrogen gas.

3CuO(s) + 2NH3 (g) ------------> 3Cu(s) 3H2O + N2 (g)
