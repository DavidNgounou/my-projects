LABORATORY PREPARATION OF CARBON DIOXIDE

Aim: laboratory preparation of CO2 by action of Dilute HCL on Calcium Carbonate.

Requirement: Flat-bottomed flask, conical flask, U-tube, gas jar, Thistle funnel, marble chips, dilute hydrochloric acid, Potassium hydrogen carbonate, anhydrous Calcium chloride.

Procedure: Place some marble chips in flat-bottomed flask. Add some dilute HCL or nitric acid to the chips through thistle funnel. Carbon dioxide gas is evolved and is first passed through KHCO3 solution to remove any acid fumes and then through a U-tube containing anhydrous CaCl2 to remove water vapor (dried the gas). The dry gas is the collected by downward delivery, since it's denser than air.

CaCO3 + 2HCL-----------> CaCl2 + CO2 H2O

Note: Sulphuric acid cannot be used in the place of hydrochloric acid because sulphuric acid reacts with chips to form an insoluble coat of Calcium Sulphate which prevents further reaction.

TEST FOR CARBONDIOXIDE

CO2 gas turns lime water (calcium hydroxide solution) milky as white precipitate of CaCO3 is formed

Ca(OH)2 + CO2 -----------> CaCO3(s) + H2

USES

  1. Used in the manufacture of sodium carbonate (washing soda) by solvay process
  2. In the manufacture of urea and ammonium sulphate. CaSO4 + CO2 + 2NH3 + H2O ---------------> (NH4)2SO4 + CaCO3
  3. Solid CO2 (dry ice) is used as a refrigerant for perishable goods
